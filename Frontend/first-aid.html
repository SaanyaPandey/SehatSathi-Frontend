SehatSathi First-Aid Assistance

Burns

Cool the burn with clean running water for at least 10 minutes.
Do not use ice, butter, or creams.
Cover the burn loosely with a clean, non-stick cloth and seek medical help if needed.

Bleeding

Apply direct pressure on the wound using a clean cloth or bandage.
If possible, elevate the injured area.
Do not remove soaked bandages—add more layers if needed.

Choking

Encourage the person to cough.
If not breathing, perform 5 back blows followed by 5 abdominal thrusts (Heimlich maneuver).
Call emergency services immediately if the object does not dislodge.

Faint

Help the person lie down flat and raise their legs slightly.
Loosen any tight clothing.
If unconscious for more than a minute, seek medical help immediately.

Snake-Bite

Keep the person calm and still.
Do not suck out venom or apply a tourniquet.
Immobilize the bitten area and seek emergency medical help immediately.

Insect / Bee Sting

Remove the stinger gently if visible (scrape, don’t pinch).
Wash the area with soap and water.
Apply a cold compress to reduce swelling.

Heart Attack

Symptoms include chest pain, breathlessness, nausea, and sweating.
Call emergency services immediately.
Help the person sit comfortably and stay calm. If available, give aspirin.

Fracture

Do not try to move or straighten the broken bone.
Immobilize the area with a splint or support.
Apply ice packs to reduce swelling and seek immediate medical help.

Electric Shock

Turn off the power source before touching the person.
Do not use bare hands.
Check for breathing and pulse. If unresponsive, begin CPR and call emergency help.

Siezure

Protect the person from injury—move nearby objects.
Do not restrain or put anything in their mouth.
After the seizure, place them on their side and check for breathing.

Fever / HeadStroke

Move the person to a cool area.
Give water in small sips and apply a cool damp cloth.
If the fever is very high or heatstroke symptoms persist, seek medical attention.

NoseBleed

Pinch the soft part of the nose and lean forward.
Do not tilt the head back.
Hold for 10 minutes and repeat if necessary. Seek help if bleeding lasts more than 20 minutes.
